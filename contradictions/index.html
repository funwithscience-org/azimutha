The Contradiction Map

Reading all thirteen papers together surfaces something no single review can: the papers do not agree with each other. This is a cross-corpus audit of the author against himself.

The pattern: he never contradicts mainstream physics politely — he demolishes it. The trouble is that he also, repeatedly, demolishes himself. Below are nineteen places where a claim, assumption, or endorsement in one paper is incompatible with one in another. Every quotation is verbatim from his own papers. The most damaging are first, and special attention is paid to cases where a later paper undercuts an earlier one — a rupture invisible while the earlier paper stood alone.

How to read this

Each entry sets two of his own passages side by side — one in navy, one in burgundy — and shows why they cannot both be true. Paper labels link to that paper's full review. A short note records whether the collision is a later paper undercutting an earlier one or a simultaneous tension running through the corpus.

01

“Gravity acts directly upon this mass”

High

He denies gravity across the corpus — then affirms it by name, and uses it as a real force, in the two papers where his argument needs it.

Paper #6 · Plants

Gravity acts directly upon this mass, generating a downward torque at the pivot point… the branch must naturally bend or sag toward the ground under its own weight. — though the paper's own caption says plants react to environment, not a magical force.

— cannot both be true —
Paper #4 · Earth Shapes

any orbit is a ballistic path requiring precise balance between centrifugal force (Fc = mv²/r) and gravitational force (Fg = GMm/r²). Oceans, likewise, conform to gravitational equipotential surfaces. Meanwhile #1, #10, #11, #12 and #13 call gravity a legal fiction and a non-existent mechanism.

Why it collidesThe load-bearing thesis of the entire corpus is that gravity does not exist and “down” is density or pressure or charge. You cannot found a project on “there is no gravity” while invoking gravity by name to explain a drooping branch and to compute the Moon's orbit.

Simultaneous tension — foundational papers #4/#6 concede gravity; the later denial capstones (#10, #12, #13) make the collision retroactively fatal.

The pattern

Who is consistent, and who is the offender

Most self-consistent

They survive by making few positive claims.

  • #2 Gravimeters and #8 Atomic Clocks — narrowly-scoped “the-instrument-measures-displacement-not-the-cause” teardowns. They stay tight precisely because they mostly withhold a cosmology; their only cross-corpus wounds are the imported photon-momentum fact (#2) and the accepted GPS satellites (#8) that his later dome papers contradict.
  • #5 Gyroscopes — same discipline; its one export (Coriolis is a non-physical pseudo-force) collides with his use of centrifugal force elsewhere, but the paper itself is coherent.
  • #11 Torsion Balance — his most technically accurate; internally disciplined, but the gloating aether line and the “G is fiction” stance are its exposed flanks.

Biggest offenders

  • #4 Earth Shapes — the single worst. It proves Earth is a “perfect sphere” using gravity and orbits, then denies it's a proven globe; it treats gravity, ocean equipotentials, satellites, parallax, and gyro/GPS reliability as real — everything the rest of the corpus abolishes.
  • #10 Aristotle — a maximalist positive cosmology (dome, plasma engines, maglev tracks, no particles, no time) that collides with nearly every instrument paper and with his own “no particles / hardware-first” rules.
  • #9 Einstein — rehabilitates relativity, the cosmological constant, and quantization (E = hv, “pixelated” energy), all of which later papers junk.
  • #13 Newton — a greatest-hits capstone; by recycling everything, it inherits every earlier collision at once.
The through-line

The further he moves from “your instrument doesn't prove causation” (defensible, narrow) toward “here is what the universe actually is” (dome, plasma, maglev, no particles, no gravity, no time), the more he collides with himself. His scoped skeptical papers are consistent because they assert almost nothing; his cosmological papers are inconsistent because they assert almost everything — including, repeatedly, the very things his other papers were written to destroy.

On method

Every quotation above is transcribed verbatim from the papers (original spelling preserved), and each paper's full review — steelman, claim-by-claim, and primary sources — is one click away through the links. A contradiction between two of an author's own claims is not, by itself, proof that either is wrong; it is proof that they cannot both be right, and that the corpus has no single coherent physics underneath it. Where we think one side is also false on the merits, that case is made on the individual review page, not here.
